For 15 years I watched how consulting typically works: a senior engineer spreads themselves across 3-4 clients, context-switching all week, giving each one maybe 60% of their actual attention. The client gets a name on a contract. They don't get the engineer.

I do it differently. When I'm with you, I'm **fully there**. No other client, no context-switching, no "let me get back to you next Tuesday because I'm at another engagement." You get 100% of my attention for the time we've agreed on.

## Why this works better

**For you:** You get someone who is actually immersed in your codebase, your team dynamics, your architecture decisions. Not someone who has to re-read their own notes every Monday to remember what they decided on Thursday.

**For me:** I can go deep. I can hold the whole system in my head. I can spot the subtle architecture issues that only surface when you've been living in the code for a week straight. That's impossible when you're splitting context across multiple projects.

**For the work:** Decisions get made faster. Technical debt gets addressed instead of deferred. Code reviews happen the same day, not the same week.

## The trade-off

Yes, this means I take fewer clients. Yes, this means I might not be available when you want me. But when I am available, you're getting someone who is actually working on your problem — not someone who is thinking about three other problems in the background.

In 7 years at BNP Paribas Fortis, I was embedded in one platform — Centric — serving 100,000+ users. I wasn't consulting on the side. I was *there*. That's how I work now too.

One client. Full commitment. Better results.
